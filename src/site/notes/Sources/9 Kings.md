This is a fun little rougelike, strategy, card game. The basic concept is each round you fight a king and then you get one of their cards. Cards can be played to create units, buildings or buff units or buildings. Then you do another defence and this repeats until you defeat a boss after which you can carry to endless mode or finish your run.

For such a simple concept there is a surprising amount of depth. Cards play off each other really well and can you can create some fun interactions with them. Endlessly buffing towers with the King of Stone has been my best strategy so far. I like how the cards from the different kings interact so seamlessly too. A lot of other similar games would lock you into a play style more for each king. Even though you aren't locked into a play style the kings feel really different to play because their bases have significantly different powers.

I have a few minor issues that I would like to see changed in the final release to make the gameplay a little smoother. Having the quick restart option on the first section of the menu would be a nice quality of life update for the UI so you don't have to go through an extra layer to restart if you are aiming for a specific strategy but don't have the correct cards or enemies. Also being able to skip the text pop ups between rounds and at the start of the run straight away would also make things smoother.

Overall a fun little indie game that already feels complete. When this is finished it looks like it'll have a fair bit more content too. It's currently worth the price but when it comes out it will be even more so.

It also runs really well on steamdeck and even though it says it is just playable I think it plays better than most verified games.
