This is one of a few games where my opinion of it is probably blinded by nostalgia and is my favourite of [[Sources/The Elder Scrolls\|The Elder Scrolls]] series. I think the quests and world are so interesting. The interactivity, the hostility of everyone you come across and the demon invasion create a great fantasy setting. This game has some of the best writing for its quests. There are also, some great unintentional funny moments where the AI was a bit overambitious for the time.

There are also some Immersive Sim like elements with the interactivity in the world, branching quests and the dynamic ways NPCs interact with each other.

I don't think this needs any mods to update the experience to the modern day but I do think you need to be aware of the levelling system, read the 'The Levelling Problem' section on the wiki here [Oblivion Levelling](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Leveling). If you aren't aware you can make your character very weak by levelling up. Another option is to adjust the difficulty slider down a few notches when enemies start to become spongy, or there is a simple mod which avoids the main issues called [All +5 Attribute Modifiers](https://www.nexusmods.com/oblivion/mods/2691).
