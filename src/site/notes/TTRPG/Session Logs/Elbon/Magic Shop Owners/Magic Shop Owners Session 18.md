**[[TTRPG/Elbon Setting/Calendar/Elbon Calendar\|Calendar]] Date:** 34-3-596
## Session Notes

### A Week in the Life of a Magic Shop Owner
![Stagbrook.png](/img/user/z_assets/TTRPG/Place%20Pictures/Stagbrook/Stagbrook.png)
- After a full week of rest the party were fully recovered.
- Maia had been practicing her clerical abilities and could now cast light for free once a day
- Breadwig had learnt how to create a painting, like the Sarzan paint, of himself that would last half an hour
- From the notes from the Sarzan that remained in the keep they found lots of scrawled writing and half finished notes. He would start a project and when it seemed like it would not achieve his goal of immortality the notes would suddenly stop. This included notes about the [[TTRPG/My Monsters/Ebonoss\|Ebonoss]].
- They were able to determine that in the keep there would likely be ghosts and doppelgängers which they had not yet encountered.
- He had notes about the change depths of the earth, where things seemed to linger for eternity, but they morphed and changed. The most prominent of these was [[TTRPG/Elbon Setting/Creatures of the Deep - On Those Things Which Refuse To End\|Creatures of the Deep - On Those Things Which Refuse To End]]
- Sarzan's notes had become more obsessive and more incomprehensible as they went.
- The party were also able to manually take notes between the middle aged and young Sarzan paintings and they had become fond pen pals
- Jatumal had being talking to young Sarzan about the great collapse. Young Sarzan was eager to talk about this but middle aged Sarzan was much less interested. He had discovered that the war had started after a dwarf commander accidentally ate the elf ruler's meal. He had also learned that before the collapse the world had much greater technologies which they had still not returned to centuries later
- Maia had been doing general good deeds and along with the others and the Stagbrook locals had been fending off attacks from Pavnutia the vampire. The vampire seemed to have taken up residence in Acman's old tower. Stagbrook were now keeping strict watch and making good use of the defences which the party had funded
- Gary had returned to report on Stonehell to Stonehaven conversion progress during the weak. The goblins and hobgoblins were now at war with the lizard people in the dungeon so progress had stalled.
- Some weird occurrences had begun to happen in Stagbrook. Doors not working, items disappearing. It seemed like the weird properties of the caves below were being to seep through to the surface.
- Everyone's unnamed magical items had stopped working. Sarzan had explained magic needed a name to work properly and everyone had begun to name their items. If they used the true names of the items, like Geog did for [[TTRPG/My Magic Items/Darkfire\|Darkfire]], then they would be even more powerful. Everyone had started to name their items. Maia and Sphonyx copied the runes from their sword to notes for Sarzan to translate in hopes of finding their true names.
### Pushing Deeper Into Stagbrook Keep
**Level 1 Map (post [[TTRPG/My Monsters/Ignid\|Ignid]] explosion):** 
![Stagbrook Keep Level 1 After Ignid Explosion.jpg](/img/user/z_assets/TTRPG/Place%20Pictures/Stagbrook/StagbrookKeep/Stagbrook%20Keep%20Level%201%20After%20Ignid%20Explosion.jpg)
**Level 2 Map (A lot of this got skipped):**
![Stagbrook_Keep_Level_2_An.png](/img/user/z_assets/TTRPG/Place%20Pictures/Stagbrook/StagbrookKeep/Stagbrook_Keep_Level_2_An.png)
- After their week of rest the party finally decided to re-enter Stagbrook Keep and deal with whatever dark things the real Sarzan had been so scared of
- They made their way down the stairs, avoided the spike trap, hopped through the room with the chopped up tables, disturbed the cave locusts for fun, and then Maia levitated everyone across the pedestal room with the huge drops. They went to the opening on the far side that had been caused by the [[TTRPG/My Monsters/Ignid\|Ignid]] explosion.
- They found a cave that gradually got thicker with webs they ventured in with Maia calling ahead with her ability to speak spider. Eventually some spiders responded, they were anxious. In part of the conversation, they explained that they ate the giant driver ants that were also living in the caves. Jatumal decided to give them his ants which he had found in the vault on the first level of the keep, Maia translated. He announced they were called Antony, and in giving them a name and opening their container activated their magical ability to clean. They began eating all the webs lightning quick.
- Jatumal quickly called them back but damaged had been done. The spiders were now even more anxious. Maia was able to calm them down and they explained that they rarely went into man made parts of the keep, but it intersected with the cave and there was a man alive but trapped in a stone box.
- The party assumed this man was the real life, unpainted Sarzan and asked for passage through to this area. The spiders begrudgingly escorted them through their colony on level 2 of the keep
- They were taken to an infinite staircase, which was only not infinite if you walked on the left-hand side of the stairs, but the spiders explained how to use it.
- Breadwig and Geog led the way down the stairs experimenting with switching between the left and right side as they went.
- Eventually the reached a hastily cut stone room with a stone coffin at the centre. They could hear a muffled breathing. Breadwig made himself invisible to go and look inside, but without the light of the party who were on the other side of the room he could only make out that there was a body inside.
- Everyone was anxious about the room. Jatumal cast detect evil. There was no evil coming from the coffin, but there was something malicious on the ceiling. It was not visible though.
- Geog decided to ignore this and began shouting over to the coffin to see if whoever was inside would respond. There was a mumbling but they couldn't make anything out that was said. Geog continued to shout.
- The ceiling creature fell from the ceiling down onto Geog dealing a devastating blow. In it's movement it brought a magical and impenetrable darkness with it. It completely encapsulated the light of their torches.
- Those with infravision could see the form well enough. These were Jutara, Jatumal, Maia. The others mostly flailed at or threw things into the darkness in hopes of hitting the creature.
- In the process Jutara named her lightning sword Galileo, Magma named his nechrome sword Chillrend and his spiked whip Sanctithorn Lash.
- The party kept swinging at the creature which made devastating multi attacked bites from many limbs. After Jatumal had taken a heavy hit from it when it was targeting him for shotting it multiple times, Jutara got a hit with her Galileo on the creature. It was shocked and scurried onto the ceiling dropping down again onto the part after recovering. They each got hits on the creature with magical weapons.
- It twitched and flailed and the magical darkness began to fade. A horrible creature with an impossible number of arms was revealed. On the palm of each hand was a many toothed mouth. The party dislodged and took some of these.
- After the battle they approached the coffin to find a recognisable but deathly looking imaged of their friend from the painting. It was Sarzan twisted and broken, barely alive but somehow undying.
